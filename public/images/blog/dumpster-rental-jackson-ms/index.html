What You Can (and Can't) Put in Your Jackson Dumpster

Understanding what materials are accepted and prohibited is crucial for a smooth rental experience and proper disposal.

Accepted Materials

Our roll-off dumpsters are perfect for a wide variety of materials, including:

* Common household junk: Furniture, toys, boxes, general trash

* Construction debris: Drywall, wood, metal, concrete, roofing shingles

* Yard waste: Branches, leaves, brush, dirt (check specific limits)

* Renovation waste: Cabinets, flooring, non-hazardous building materials

* Appliances: Refrigerators, washers, dryers (ensure all refrigerants are removed)

Prohibited Items & Why

For safety, environmental protection, and compliance with local regulations, certain items cannot be placed in our dumpsters. These prohibited items include:

* Hazardous Waste: Materials like paints, solvents, thinners, oils, asbestos, car batteries, and medical waste pose significant environmental and health risks.

* Tires: Due to their unique composition, tires are difficult to compact and dispose of in landfills.

* Refrigerants: Appliances containing refrigerants (like old refrigerators or AC units) must have these harmful chemicals professionally removed before disposal.

* Flammable Liquids & Explosives: Any materials that are combustible or could explode are strictly prohibited due to extreme safety hazards.

* Asbestos: This material is a serious health hazard and requires highly regulated handling by certified professionals.

* Biohazardous Materials: Items that could transmit disease, such as medical waste, must be handled with specialized protocols.

If you're unsure about a specific item, please don't hesitate to call us. We're here to help you navigate junk disposal safely and responsibly.

Navigating Dumpster Permits in Jackson, MS

Understanding when and if you need a permit for your dumpster rental in Jackson, MS is an important step in the process.

Do You Need a Permit?

Generally, if you plan to place your dumpster entirely on private property (like your driveway), you will not need a permit. However, a permit is typically required if the dumpster will be placed on public property, such as:

* A city street

* A public sidewalk

* Any other public right-of-way

Jackson, MS Permit Guidelines

For specific permit guidelines, you will need to contact the City of Jackson's Public Works department. They can provide the most current information on application processes, fees, and requirements for placing a roll-off container on public property. We recommend checking with them well in advance of your rental period to ensure a smooth process. While we cannot obtain permits for you, we can offer guidance on where to look.

The overall rental cost is primarily influenced by:

* Dumpster Size: Larger dumpsters typically have higher rental fees.

* Rental Period: Standard rental periods (e.g., 7 days) are usually included, but extended periods may incur additional charges.

* Type of Debris: Certain materials, like heavy inert debris (concrete, dirt), may have different pricing structures.

* Tonnage Limits: Each dumpster rental comes with a pre-set weight limit.

* Overage Fees: Exceeding the included weight limit will result in additional fees.

Weight Overage Prevention & Management

Staying within your weight limits is key to managing prices. Here are some practical tips:

* Estimate Your Debris: For construction debris, a good rule of thumb is that a cubic yard of general waste weighs about 1,000-1,500 pounds. For household junk, estimate based on the volume and density of items.

* Load Efficiently: Break down bulky items. This not only saves space but can also help distribute weight evenly.

* Be Aware of Heavy Materials: Common materials that are unexpectedly heavy include dirt, concrete, bricks, and roofing shingles. If your project involves a lot of these, consider a smaller dumpster dedicated to heavy debris.

* Ask Us for Help: If you’re unsure, give us a call! We can help you estimate your debris weight and recommend the right size for your cleanout to avoid overage fees.
